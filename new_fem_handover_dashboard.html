What to keep in mind

  • Forms feed directly into the Welcome Series trigger — any changes to form targeting or list assignment will affect flow entry
  • The Instant Audiences pool operates on a separate list trigger; sign-up form changes should be checked against both entry points

Voice

Warm, feminine, and confidence-led. The audience is a broad age range of Australian women shopping for everyday wear, occasion wear, and maternity-friendly pieces. Copy reads like a stylish friend who already knows what suits you — not a stylist talking down, and not a hype brand using urgency tricks.

  • Second person (you, your) — never "customers" or "shoppers" in body copy
  • First-name personalisation in subject lines and body where natural; fallback to "lovely" or "there"
  • Australian English everywhere — favourite, colour, organise, personalise
  • Emojis used sparingly — pink heart, sparkle, flower — usually one per subject line maximum

Design system

  • Primary green #1F4D43 — backgrounds, headlines on light, primary CTA
  • Accent pink #D17A92 — hearts, accents, secondary CTA
  • Warm beige #C9BDA7 — soft backgrounds and tags
  • All-caps display headers with generous letter spacing
  • Pink and beige hearts as a signature motif — used sparingly in seasonal headers
  • Hero ratio standard 600px wide; approved mobile and desktop variants per template
Approved mobile and desktop hero designs exist for every flow. The recurring optimisation note across A/B reports is: apply approved designs across every touchpoint. Do not deviate without re-approval.

During major promotions, standard abandonment flows include a 10% discount code. To prevent customers shopping at sale prices from receiving a further discount on top — which caused complaints in the past — a set of cloned "WITHOUT DISCOUNT" variants has been built and sits in Klaviyo drafts ready to activate.

How the swap works

  • Outside sale periods: standard (discounted) variants are live
  • At the start of a major promo: pause the standard variants, activate the WITHOUT DISCOUNT ones using a Klaviyo timer
  • After the sale ends: revert — re-activate standard variants, pause WITHOUT DISCOUNT ones
The swap is manual — no automation toggles these. Set calendar reminders for the day before the sale opens and the day it ends. Watch the Loom walkthrough below for step-by-step timer instructions.

A/B testing was treated as a continuous practice, not a campaign-specific one. Every major flow has been tested at least once, with winners documented in the monthly reporting deck.

Test variables

  • Subject line — the most common test
  • Send time — 8:30am vs 10:30am vs 1pm AEST tested across multiple flows
  • Content angle (urgency vs social proof vs style-led)
  • Discount inclusion (with / without)

Statistical rules

  • Run for at least 30 days; high-volume flows extended beyond
  • Use Klaviyo's built-in win probability, not just point estimates
  • Conversion-based metrics (placed order, revenue) override engagement when calling a winner

Yotpo vs Klaviyo — who sends what

To avoid duplicate sends, transactional loyalty messages (points earned, reminders, tier earned, birthday reward, referral share) send from Yotpo. Everything else — welcome, abandonment, promo campaigns, post-purchase — sends from Klaviyo.
